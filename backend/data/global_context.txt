Dutch EdTech EcosystemConnecting the EdTech community nationwide to accelerate learning and education innovationThe State of theOctober 2025
JITSKE van OS Managing Director Dutch Edtech Foundation
EWOUD de KOK Chairman Board Dutch Edtech Foundation CEO / Founder FeedbackFruits Foreword2Why this report The strength of the Dutch knowledge economy depends on our collective capacity to learn. Only by expanding that capacity can we successfully navigate today’s major transitions. EdTech plays a pivotal role: it drives learning innovation, expands our knowledge base, and secures the future strength of the Dutch economy. Are we building enough momentum for startups to scale?  This is a critical challenge for the sector. Without a strong EdTech ecosystem, the Netherlands risks losing both innovation power and digital sovereignty. Dominant global tech players are making a massive push into education with AI and other technologies. If we don’t strengthen our own ecosystem, we risk becoming overly dependent on them - losing influence over how we learn, work, and develop.  By investing in our own strength, we can position ourselves as a strong partner in European and global collaboration. Every societal transition, whether in healthcare, energy, or the labor market, is fundamentally a learning transition. EdTech provides the essential infrastructure that makes learning accessible, adaptive, and scalable.  This report is therefore more than just numbers. It sends a strong message: the Netherlands must act now to invest in a resilient EdTech ecosystem. With the right conditions, the Netherlands has the right launchpad for entrepreneurs to deliver real impact at scale. But they need a fair chance!

Executive summary.Executive summary.3The Dutch EdTech sector shows  a strong growth & maturity. The Dutch EdTech sector has quadrupled in five years, reaching nearly $2 billion in market value. The number of companies grew by +20% to over 500, and jobs expanded by +18% to 13,400 FTEs. EdTech has moved from hype to becoming a sustainable pillar of the Dutch knowledge economy. Lifelong Learning is the  growth engine of the sector. Over half of Dutch EdTech companies focus on lifelong learning and higher education. These segments dominate in scale, employment, and innovation, while primary and secondary education remain fragmented and small-scale. Companies face structural  barriers to scaling. Despite a dense ecosystem, no new OECD/EU-defined scaleups have emerged. Startups struggle mainly with go-to-market strategies, while scale-ups face challenges in international expansion and talent acquisition. This limits the sector’s ability to grow globally. There is a funding crisis &  ineffective public support. Since 2024, venture capital has collapsed: Series B/C rounds disappeared entirely, and average deal size in the Netherlands plunged to $1M (versus $3M in the EU). While numerous public funding initiatives exist, they appear insufficiently effective, as the pipeline from startup to scale-up remains weak. This fragile funding landscape increases dependency on foreign big tech players. Strategic breakthrough momentum,  opportunities and urgency. 70% of Dutch EdTech companies now integrate AI, and 85% are planning international expansion (with Germany as the top target market). EdTech is crucial to solving societal transitions. From education gaps to energy and workforce transformation. But only with targeted support (public, private, and European) can the Netherlands safeguard digital sovereignty and position itself as a global EdTech leader.
