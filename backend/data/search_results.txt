Energy Transition Skills.
37Societal Challenges - Energy Transition SkillsThe rapid shift to green energy in the Netherlands is creating a significant skills gap. Employers struggle to find technicians, installers, and specialists for solar, wind, grid, and circular technologies, while traditional training remains too slow and rigid. Beyond technical roles, expertise in circularity and sustainable supply chain practices is increasingly essential. Demand for green skills rises faster than supply, and current workforce readiness falls short of meeting the country’s energy transition goals1. EdTech as a solution ๏Targeted training – Digital certification programmes and role-specific learning modules prepare new technicians and installers to be job-ready from day one. ๏Scalable delivery – Online platforms, VR/AR simulations, and multilingual e-learning make it possible to train large groups quickly, overcoming the limits of traditional classrooms. ๏Future-proof knowledge – Open digital academies provide accessible courses on hydrogen, system integration, and energy innovation, ensuring continuous reskilling and interdisciplinary expertise for long-term transformation.
